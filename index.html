I took a walk on the Clovis Old Town Trail

The Clovis Old Town Trail is a paved pedestrian path that follows the old narrow gauge railroad route that used to the serve the stone fruit farms north of Old Town Clovis. The tracks got torn out and converted into a pedestrian path. It is perfect for any pedestrian activity, like walking, jogging and bicycling. There is beyond ample parking at the Sierra Vista Mall.

History Along The Old Town Trail

With educational displays along the trail, valley history comes alive. More displays are getting added all the time. This display is about an important group of immigrants connected to this walking path, the Japanese. Their stone fruit farms north of Clovis were served by the railroad track which got converted into this trail. The track was the first strech of the fresh fruits' journey to world markets. Long time valley residents might recall the Takahashi Farms fruit stand.

The More Time Marches On, The More Classic Things Become

Along this walk sits parked this beauty. Classic cars show off the automotive design of the past, which captures all the modern design trends that have gone by, such as the speed lines of 1940s and "space age" fins of the 1950s. This beast was parked out front of a great local microbrewery, Tactical Ops, owned by a group of young veterans who like beer.

Next I passed by the Rodeo Grounds

The famous Clovis Rodeo is one of the biggest annual events in the San Joaquin Valley. Ropers and riders from the circuit rounding the country stop by every spring to compete in all the rodeo events. The grounds are located where the no-longer-existent Pine Ridge to Clovis log flume terminated. Logs from the Sierras would ride the flume all the way to a sawmill, which now is the Clovis Rodeo Grounds.

Eventually I arrived in Old Town

It's a bit off its game because of the pandemic, but Old Town Clovis is a fantastic rest stop. It has bars, restaurants, and a poker room that has been there since the 1950s.
